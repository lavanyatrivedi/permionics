What's the difference between UF, NF, and RO? Which one do I need?
▼
Think of it as filtration by pore size:
  • Ultrafiltration (UF): Removes suspended solids, bacteria, viruses (about 0.01 microns). Good for pre-treatment.
  • Nanofiltration (NF): Removes smaller molecules like hardness, organic compounds (about 0.001 microns). Middle ground.
  • Reverse Osmosis (RO): Removes dissolved salts and almost all contaminants (about 0.0001 microns). For highest purity.
We typically combine these in multi-stage systems because they work better together. For example: UF arrow RO arrow EDI gives you ultra-pure water. We analyze your specific water challenge and design accordingly.
What's Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD) and why should I care?
▼
ZLD means nothing goes to drain - every drop of wastewater is either recovered for reuse or converted to solid waste (dry sludge). It sounds extreme, but it's increasingly a regulatory requirement and a major cost-saver. Instead of paying for wastewater discharge (which is increasingly expensive), you recover 80-95% of water for reuse. Our Waaree solar client recovered 10 MLD water daily - that's 3.65 million liters annually. No discharge penalties, lower freshwater bills.

What's the difference between "ultrapure water" and just RO water?
▼
  • RO water: About 0.5-5 Mega Ohms (good for cooling, general process)
  • High-purity water: 10-14 Mega Ohms (semiconductors, pharma formulation)
  • Ultra-pure water: 15-18+ Mega Ohms (solar wafers, advanced electronics)
The difference is operational complexity and cost. Ultra-pure requires EDI + polishing, which is more expensive but essential for wafer quality. Regular RO won't cut it if your wafer yield depends on less than 2 PPB ionic contaminants.
How do you handle silica and fluoride? (Common in semiconductor & solar feed water)
▼
Silica removal is tricky because silica can pass through RO if not managed. We use specialized silica-selective membranes + chemical conditioning to precipitate silica before RO. Fluoride is handled via dedicated ion-exchange resins in the polishing stage. Solar & semiconductor feeds are our specialty - these contaminants are expected, and our system design accounts for them.

What's this "Low Fouling RO" membrane I've heard about?

What's the difference between spiral-wound and hollow-fiber membranes?
▼
  • Spiral-wound (RO/NF): Flat sheet sandwiched in a spiral. High pressure, good for recovery systems, excellent for TDS removal
  • Hollow-fiber (UF): Thin tubes. Lower pressure, good for pre-treatment, excellent for particulates
We use both. UF as pre-treatment (hollow-fiber), then RO for final polishing (spiral-wound). Each excels at its job.
How do you prevent or manage membrane fouling?
▼
Prevention is better than cure. Our approach:
  1. Right pre-treatment: Remove foulants before they reach membrane
  2. Membrane selection: Low-fouling chemistry for dirty streams
  3. Operational discipline: Right pressure, pH, temperature settings
  4. Cleaning schedule: Proactive cleaning before fouling worsens
  5. Additives: Anti-scalant in feed to prevent salt deposits
Our Turbocatch pre-treatment (UF-based) is engineered to reduce fouling by 50+ % vs sand filters.
